Information

The Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya Selection Test (JNVST) is an entrance exam for admission to Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalayas which provide Free Residential Education from Class 6th to 12th (if exams are taken in Class 5th) or from Class 10th to 12th (if the exams are taken in Class 9th) :

Purpose: The JNVST is a merit-based test to select talented children from rural areas for admission to Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalayas.

Subjects: The JNVST covers different subjects depending on the class for which admission is sought:
Class 6: Mental ability skills, mathematics, and regional language
Class 9: English, Hindi, mathematics, and general science

Format: The JNVST is an objective test.

Frequency: The JNVST is held annually at the national, block, and district levels.

Eligibility: Candidates must meet the age criteria and pass the previous class.

Admission: Admission is based on merit, with reservations for students from rural areas, Scheduled Castes (SCs), Scheduled Tribes (STs), girls, and disabled candidates.

Examiner: The Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) designs, develops, and conducts the JNVST.
